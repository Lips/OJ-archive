Companies as victims

Interestingly enough.. the sites that are linked via spam seem to be scams - they're selling counterfeit products.
Google "supra fake" and the 3rd link is a page warning people about sites selling counterfeit goods.

There was an interesting article on Chinese counterfeits in the NY times recently.

It's a big article, but I seem to recall someone being quoted as saying that the fakes can be as high or higher quality than the originals, which is completely believable.. considering our throwaway culture, not to mention my own experience with "real" shoes being total crap.

There's an interesting concept I just read about regarding crime: Criminals, not beholden to morals or laws, exploit & expose imbalances in our world. When scrap metal prices rise high enough, you have people stealing manhole covers. The covers themselves are worth $5, but the replacement cost for the city is $2000+ when it's all added up. So the city relies on a certain balance to cheaply cover holes in the street, and other folks rely on another balance to earn a quick buck.

Crime or not, if you're willing to look deeper, it can give a glimpse into an imbalance or inflation. And it's a pretty objective glimpse, because it *works*.
